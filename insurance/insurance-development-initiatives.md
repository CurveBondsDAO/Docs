# Insurance Development Initiatives

### Current Initiatives

Curve Bonds DAO has formally engaged with leading DeFi insurance providers:

* Nexus Mutual
* InsurAce

We have submitted requests to introduce new insurance cover types tailored to the risks present in our strategies.

***

### Proposed Insurance Covers

#### 1. ETH Derivative Depeg Protection

We have applied for coverage on the following assets:

* **msETH**
* **alETH**

These covers are designed to provide protection in scenarios where an ETH derivative significantly deviates from ETH (a “depeg”).

Proposed structure:

* Parametric trigger (e.g., asset trades below a defined % of ETH)
* Sustained deviation requirement
* Oracle-based pricing (TWAP or equivalent)

This type of coverage would help mitigate losses arising from:

* Liquidity imbalances
* Market dislocations
* Structural instability in derivative markets

***

#### 2. LlamaLend Bad Debt Coverage

We have also proposed a new class of cover focused on:

> **Bad debt in LlamaLend markets, regardless of cause**

This includes scenarios such as:

* Failed or insufficient liquidations
* Rapid collateral price movements
* Liquidity shortages
* Cascading market events

This type of risk is currently **uninsured across DeFi**, and we are working with providers to explore whether it can be structured into a viable and bounded coverage product.

***

#### 3. Market-Specific Bad Debt Coverage (Alternative)

If generalized bad debt coverage is not feasible, we have proposed narrower coverage scoped to specific markets, such as:

* sfrxETH / sfrxUSD (Fraxtal)
* asdCRV (Arbitrum)

This approach may allow for:

* More precise risk modeling
* Bounded exposure
* Improved underwriting feasibility

***

### Participation & Liquidity Commitment

Curve Bonds DAO is not only seeking coverage — we are committed to **bootstrapping and supporting these markets**.

If approved, the DAO will:

* Allocate treasury capital to **seed and underwrite insurance pools**
* Provide additional incentives for the community to participate as underwriters
* Act as a primary user of the coverage within treasury strategies

This ensures:

* Immediate demand for the product
* Sufficient underwriting capacity
* Long-term sustainability of the cover markets

***

### Limitations & Reality

Not all risks in DeFi are currently insurable.

In particular:

* Economic risks (e.g., bad debt, liquidity failure) are difficult to underwrite
* Custom assets (e.g., ETH derivatives) may require new market creation
* Coverage capacity may be limited even if approved

As such, insurance is treated as a **supplementary layer**, not a complete solution.

***

### Internal Risk Management

Where external insurance is unavailable, Curve Bonds DAO implements:

* Active monitoring of ETH derivative pegs
* Dynamic allocation strategies
* Dedicated liquidation infrastructure
* Reserve buffers funded by protocol yield

These systems are designed to reduce exposure to:

* Depegs
* Insolvency events
* Market dislocations

***

### Future Outlook

If our proposed covers are approved, Curve Bonds DAO will become one of the first protocols to integrate:

* **Custom depeg insurance for ETH derivatives**
* **Potential coverage for lending market insolvency risk**

This represents a significant step toward:

> **Structured, risk-defined DeFi yield products**

We will continue to iterate on both external and internal protection mechanisms as the DeFi insurance landscape evolves.
